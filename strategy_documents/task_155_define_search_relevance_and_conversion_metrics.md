# Detailed Strategy Document: Define Search Relevance and Conversion Metrics

## Product Problem Statement
The current supermarket search experience lacks relevance and efficiency, leading to poor customer discovery and low search-led conversion rates.

## Target Users
- Supermarket customers
- Online grocery shoppers
- Supermarket management teams

## User Needs
- Accurate and relevant search results
- Quick discovery of desired products
- Personalized search experiences
- Efficient navigation through product categories

## Product Recommendation
Implement an AI-driven search algorithm using Algolia to enhance search relevance and personalization. Integrate real-time inventory updates to ensure accurate product availability information. Develop a user-friendly interface that allows for easy filtering and sorting of search results.

## 30/60/90 Day Execution Plan
### 30-Day Plan (Phase 1: Setup & Alignment)
- Focus action: Conduct A/B testing of the new search algorithm to measure impact on conversion rates
- Establish search catalog sync and index configuration.
- Align technical stakeholders on success indicators.

### 60-Day Plan (Phase 2: Integration & Beta Launch)
- Focus action: Gather continuous customer feedback post-implementation to refine search features
- Implement search layout widgets and facet filters in development components.
- Launch internal beta to evaluate search responsiveness and accuracy.

### 90-Day Plan (Phase 3: Launch & Scale)
- Focus action: Monitor and analyze search data to identify areas for further improvement
- Go live with production indexes and scale traffic.
- Monitor search click-through analytics and optimize relevancy filters.

## Success Metrics
- Increase in search-led conversion rate by 20% within 6 months
- Reduction in search abandonment rate by 15%
- Improvement in customer satisfaction scores related to search experience by 25%
- Increase in average time spent on the search page by 10%

## Risk Assessment & Mitigations
- Potential data privacy concerns with AI-driven personalization
- Technical challenges in integrating real-time inventory updates
- High implementation costs for AI and Algolia integration
- Resistance to change from users accustomed to the current system

## Key Assumptions
- Users prefer personalized search experiences
- Real-time inventory updates will improve customer satisfaction
- AI-driven search will significantly enhance search relevance
- Customers are willing to adapt to a new search interface

## Evidence & References
- Customer feedback from surveys and focus groups
- Competitor analysis reports on Walmart and Amazon Fresh
- Technical feasibility studies on real-time inventory integration
- Cost-benefit analysis of AI-driven personalization
